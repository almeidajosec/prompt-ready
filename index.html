The competitive landscape

AI is choosing winners
and losers. Right now.

Right now, someone is asking ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI for a recommendation in your industry. The AI generates one answer, cites two to seven sources — and your competitor's name appears. Yours doesn't.

It's not that your competitor has a better product. It's that their website is formatted so that AI can extract, verify, and cite it with confidence. Yours — however well-optimized for Google — isn't.

The rankings for the AI era are being set right now. Early movers will be extremely difficult to displace.

Users who discover a business through an AI citation convert at up to 3× the rate of traditional search visitors. They arrive with context, specific intent, and pre-built trust. This isn't a future problem. It's a current one.

Why they're winning

The three things your
competitors are doing differently.

None require a content overhaul. All are implementable once you know where the gaps are.

1

Their content answers directly

AI engines favor content that gives the answer in the first 50 words, uses lists and tables, and structures headings as questions. Sites that do this get cited. Sites that bury the answer get skipped.

2

Their brand is a recognized entity

Schema markup translates your website into the language AI engines understand — who you are, what you do, why you're credible. Without it, you're an anonymous block of text competing with one that has a clear identity.

3

AI can verify their authority

E-E-A-T signals — author credentials, citations from recognized domains, mentions in communities like Reddit and Quora — are the credentials AI checks before citing a source. Your competitors have them visible. You may not.

The assessment

See exactly where you stand.

We analyze your website across the same 4 dimensions AI engines use to decide what to recommend — and give you a prioritized plan to close the gaps.

Answer-Shaped Content

  • → Is your answer in the first 50 words?
  • → Are Q&A headings (H2/H3) used correctly?
  • → Are lists, tables, and structured formats in place?
  • → Do you have original, citable data or statistics?

Technical AI Foundation

  • → Organization Schema present and validated?
  • → Article / BlogPosting markup on content pages?
  • → FAQPage Schema for question-answer content?
  • → Brand entity consistent and clearly defined?

Authority & Trust Signals

  • → Detailed author bios with visible credentials?
  • → Citations from recognized external publications?
  • → Brand mentions in Reddit, Quora, or niche forums?
  • → E-E-A-T signals visible and machine-readable?

Crawlability & Access

  • → Site speed and HTTPS status
  • → Crawl waste and unnecessary indexation blockers
  • → Last-updated dates visible on content pages
  • → No technical signals blocking AI crawlers
